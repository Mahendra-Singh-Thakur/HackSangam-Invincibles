1.The Pradhan Mantri fasal bima yojana (PMFBY)

Scheme Description:

The pradhan mantri fasal beema yojana is launched on 18 February 2016 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi is an insurance service for farmers for their yields.[1] It was formulated in line with One Nation–One Scheme theme by replacing earlier two schemes National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS) and Modified National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (MNAIS) by incorporating their best features and removing their inherent drawbacks (shortcomings). It aims to reduce the premium burden on farmers and ensure early settlement of crop assurance claim for the full insured sum.

Objective of the Scheme:

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) aims at supporting sustainable production in agriculture sector by way of[3]
Providing financial support to farmers suffering crop loss/damage arising out of unforeseen events Stabilizing the income of farmers to ensure their continuance in farming Encouraging farmers to adopt innovative and modern agricultural practices Ensuring flow of credit to the agriculture sector which will contribute to food security, crop diversification, and enhancing growth and competitiveness

2.Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

Scheme Description:

Pradhan mantri kisan samman nidhi is a Central Sector scheme with 100% funding from Government of India. The Scheme is effective from 1.12.2018. Under the Scheme an income support of Rs.6000/- per year is provided to all farmer families across the country in three equal installments of Rs.2000/- each every four months. Definition of family for the Scheme is husband, wife and minor children.

Objective of the Scheme:

The entire responsibility of identification of beneficiary farmer families rests with the State / UT Governments. The fund is directly transferred to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries. Farmers covered under the Exclusion Criteria of the Operational Guidelines are not eligible for the benefit of the Scheme.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana

Scheme Description:

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana is a government scheme meant for old age protection and social security of Small and Marginal Farmers (SMF). All Small and Marginal Farmers having cultivable landholdings of up to 2 hectares falling in the age group of 18 to 40 years, whose names appear in the land records of states and UTs as of August 1, 2019, are eligible to get benefits under the Scheme. Under this scheme, the farmers would receive a minimum assured pension of ₹ 3000/- per month after attaining the age of 60 years and if the farmer dies, the spouse of the farmer shall be entitled to receive 50% of the pension as a family pension. Family pension applies only to spouses.

4. Agri-Export Promotion Scheme (AEPS):

Scheme Description:

AEPS facilitates the export of agricultural commodities by providing financial assistance, infrastructure support, quality certification, and market promotion activities.

Objective of the Scheme:

To boost agricultural exports, increase farmers' income, and integrate Indian agriculture into the global market.

5. Weather-based Crop Insurance Scheme (WBCIS):


Scheme Description:

WBCIS provides insurance coverage to farmers against weather-related risks such as drought, flood, cyclone, and unseasonal rainfall. Insurance premiums are subsidized, and claims are based on predefined weather parameters.

Objective of the Scheme:

To mitigate farmers' financial losses due to adverse weather events and encourage risk management in agriculture.

6. Integrated Farming System (IFS) Scheme:

Scheme Description:

IFS promotes the adoption of integrated farming practices that combine crop cultivation with livestock rearing, poultry farming, aquaculture, and agroforestry. Support is provided for setting up integrated farming units and training farmers in integrated farming techniques.

Objective of the Scheme:

To enhance farm productivity, optimize resource utilization, and improve farmers' income through diversified and sustainable farming systems.

7.Minimum Support Price MSP for Crops:

Scheme Description:

The Minimum Support Price (MSP) for Crops is a government-initiated program aimed at providing price security to farmers for their agricultural produce. Under this scheme, the government announces a minimum price for various crops before the planting season begins. This price acts as a floor price, ensuring that farmers receive a fair and remunerative price for their harvest.

Objective of the Scheme:

The primary objective of the MSP scheme is to safeguard the interests of farmers and ensure their economic well-being. By guaranteeing a minimum price for their produce, the government aims to protect farmers from market fluctuations and provide them with a stable source of income.
